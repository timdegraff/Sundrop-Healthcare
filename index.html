Health insurance in 60 seconds

New to all this? These are the only 5 words you need to know.

Premium

The monthly bill just to have insurance. On Plan 1, Sundrop pays it — $0 from your paycheck.

Deductible

What you pay yourself first each year before insurance starts covering big bills.

Copay

A small flat fee when you go — like $5 for a generic drug or $20 for a doctor visit.

Coinsurance

After your deductible, the share of a big bill you still pay (like 20%). 0% means insurance covers it all.

Out-of-pocket max

The most you could pay in a year. Hit it, and insurance covers 100% after.

How Sundrop pays

Sundrop puts the same dollars toward either plan. Plan 1 = fully covered ($0). On Gold, you pay just the difference.

Scenario B

A bigger year, with family

Now say you cover a spouse and two kids, and it's a busy medical year: a hospital birth, one ER trip for a kid, 4 children's visits, a couple of grown-up visits, and several prescriptions.

Remember: your own coverage is still free on Plan 1. The big numbers below come from covering your whole family — adding a spouse and kids is paid by you, at the per-person rate, on either plan. This family example uses a ~30-year-old; the age slider up top doesn't change it, since your spouse and kids are priced at their own ages.

Good to know — for families
  • •Your kids may qualify for free or low-cost Michigan coverage (Medicaid / MIChild), depending on household income.
  • •Your spouse may be able to buy a discounted Obamacare plan (the old "family glitch" was fixed in 2023).
  • •On Gold G121, a hospital birth runs through its $1,200 deductible with 0% coinsurance after. On the free Bronze plan it runs to the $8,000 deductible.

Every family is different — confirm eligibility at HealthCare.gov or Michigan's MI Bridges. Figures use 2026 guidelines and are estimates.

In short

Two plans. Your choice.

The free Bronze plan is $0 to you, with a higher deductible. The Gold plan costs a bit more each month but adds small copays and a much lower deductible. Covering a spouse or kids is your cost on either plan — and they may qualify for Medicaid or Obamacare. Either way, your own coverage can be completely free.
